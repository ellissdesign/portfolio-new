Designing with AI

Building a personal toolkit of AI agents and skills that absorb the repetitive parts of design-system work — so the craft gets the attention instead.

2025 — 26
Role
Designer · AI Tooling

Custom Agents & Skills
Overview

AI as a
design partner

Working on the NEO multi-brand design system means a lot of repeatable, detail-heavy work — component specs, token mapping across brands, accessibility audits, and research templates. I started building custom AI agents and skills to carry that load.

The goal was never to replace design judgement, but to clear the boilerplate around it so more of my time goes to the decisions that actually matter.

Each agent encodes how I actually work — my conventions, my guardrails, my definition of done — so the output already fits the NEO system rather than being generic.

The result is a workflow where I direct and refine rather than start from a blank page, and where quality stays consistent no matter how repetitive the task.

The Agents

Specialists for
each discipline

01

Multi-Brand Design System Designer

Designs components, defines variants and props, authors tokens, and enforces brand governance across the six NEO brands.

02

Design Documentation Writer

Structures component specs, usage guidelines, handoff notes, and changelogs in a consistent, reusable voice.

03

NEO Usability Test Specialist

Plans moderated and unmoderated tests, builds FigJam research templates, and synthesises findings into clear insights.

04

Web Designer

Builds and audits front-end and portfolio work with semantic HTML, design tokens, and WCAG accessibility in mind.

The Skills

Reusable
capabilities

Where agents are the specialists, skills are the focused procedures they reach for — each one a packaged way of doing a single job to the NEO standard, every time.

They turn tacit knowledge — naming rules, alias chains, severity scales, contrast thresholds — into something repeatable that doesn't depend on me remembering every detail.

S1

Component Spec Writer

Anatomy, states, variants, spacing, behaviour, and token mapping — with a per-brand manifestation section.

S2

Brand Governance Audit

Checks naming conventions, alias chain integrity, mode completeness, and cross-brand consistency.

S3

Figma Variables Builder

Builds a Primitive → Semantic → Component token architecture with modes for multi-brand theming.

S4

Accessibility Audit

Reviews components against WCAG 2.2 AA and AAA — contrast, focus, target size, and keyboard support.

S5

FigJam Template Builder

Generates structured research boards — observer grids, affinity maps, and synthesis layouts.

S6

Findings Synthesiser

Clusters raw observations into severity-rated findings with cross-brand pattern flags and recommendations.
